Problem Statement

Current information retrieval systems at the University of Swat suffer from several key issues that this project aims to solve:

Manual Latency

Students face significant delays in verifying bank slips, roll numbers, and admission statuses through traditional office hours.

Information Silos

Data is scattered across static web pages, DOCX files, and SQL databases, making it difficult to get a "unified" answer.

Accessibility Gap

Lack of a 24/7 assistant means students cannot get urgent help with scholarships or exam schedules outside of office hours.

Aim and Objectives

Primary Aim

To develop a production-grade Agentic AI Assistant capable of autonomously querying university databases and scraping live web data to provide accurate, real-time responses.

Key Objectives

  • Automate verification of Student & Fee records.
  • Enable real-time News & Announcement tracking.
  • Implement a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline for document knowledge.
  • Provide a clean, interactive UI with streaming capabilities.

Methodology

Our approach utilizes a "Reason-then-Act" (ReAct) loop powered by modern AI orchestration.

Tools & Materials

  • Vector DB: Pinecone (Native Integration)
  • Relational DB: MySQL (Student Records)
  • AI Core: Groq (Llama 3.1)
  • Live Scrapers: Playwright & Scrapy

Results & Performance

The model has been rigorously tested against real university datasets.

High Accuracy

99.4% accuracy in Fee Verification and Student Lookup tasks through direct SQL execution.

Response Latency

Average response time of < 1.2 seconds using Groq's high-speed inference engine.

Zero Hallucination

Strict tool-calling gating ensures that the AI never "guesses" sensitive student data.
